Powder River Basin

Look left +2:55 in Clear skies

A large, square dark patch in the open plains, off to the north.

It looks like nothing from 35,000 feet, but you're looking at the single largest coal-producing region in the United States. These mines supply roughly 40% of the country's coal.

The seams here run up to 200 feet thick and sit close to the surface, so it's cheaper to dig here than almost anywhere on Earth. A hundred million years ago this was a swampy coastal plain at the edge of a shallow sea.
