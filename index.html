Inside the app

More sky, less screen

Sitting 35,000 feet up, looking down on the world, is something people dreamed about for thousands of years. We take it for granted now, heads down, window shades drawn. It's still a special moment. A short glance out the window, then the phone goes back in your pocket. Not another screen to stare at for three hours.

Why offline matters

In-flight WiFi is slow, spotty, or costs extra, when it works at all. Everything here is ready before you board, so the plane's connection is never part of the plan.

Ideas we're exploring

Early thinking, not promises. If one of these is the reason you'd actually use it, that's exactly what we want to hear.

Pick your interests

Tune the timeline to what you care about: economics (the mine below that supplies a huge share of the country's coal), the spots where real history happened, natural wonders, or only the truly can't-miss, plainly visible sights.

A star map for night flights

When it's dark outside, swap landmarks for a small map of the constellations you're flying under.

Dial in the subtlety

Decide how often it speaks up, from a single nudge for the whole flight to every notable thing you pass.
